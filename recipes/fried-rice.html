Fried Rice

Steaming Fried Rice

Fried rice is a beloved dish originating from Chinese cuisine, known for its savory flavor and versatility. Made by stir-frying cooked rice with a mix of ingredients like vegetables, proteins (such as chicken, shrimp, or tofu), and seasoned with soy sauce, garlic, and ginger, it transforms leftovers into a satisfying meal.

Celebrated for its adaptability, fried rice can be customized to suit various tastes and dietary preferences. Often garnished with green onions or sesame seeds, it’s a convenient and delicious option, whether served as a main course or a side dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old for best texture)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or sesame oil)
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 green onions, sliced
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Protein of choice (chicken, shrimp, or tofu) - optional

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:

    • Make sure your cooked rice is chilled and clump-free. If using leftover rice, break up any clumps with your hands.

  2. Cook the Eggs:

    • Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the beaten eggs, scrambling until fully cooked. Remove them from the pan and set aside.

  3. Stir-Fry the Vegetables:

    • In the same pan, add another tablespoon of oil. Add the diced onion and garlic, sautéing until fragrant. Then add the mixed vegetables and stir-fry for a few minutes until tender.

  4. Combine Ingredients:

    • Add the cooked rice to the pan, breaking up any remaining clumps. Stir-fry for a few minutes until heated through. Add the cooked eggs back in, along with the soy sauce, sesame oil (if using), and green onions. Mix well and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Serve:

    • Remove from heat and enjoy your homemade fried rice!
